About Me:

Hello, my name is Anna, I'm 73 years old. Originally from Ukraine but today I live in Beer Sheva.

My hobbies are: Gardening, cooking, painting and hanging out with my family.

Through my life I worked as a manicurist, then when I got older I went to work at the construction.

Back then the soviet union (~1960~) was at it's best period, so all the young people from the villeges were helping in the construction of the new cities.

I have two children, and 4 grandkids.

When I had my first child, I started to work at the ironing factory. Even though I worked hard, I still don't think I worked as hard as my parents.

Inspired me...

My mother and father are the people I admired all my life. We lived in the village, so there was a big houshold to maintain. My parents would wake up before the sun, and go to sleep after the sun has set. My mother would wake up every morning at 3 AM, cook us food and then go to work. My father would wake up early and go to work at the field, take care of the cows, the goat and the chickens. I always admired them for their hard work.

Use case

The use case: Find information about a known bug that exists in a program and it's solution.

In Web 1.0 the user enters the website and all he can do is to read the information about the bug this website gives him. Also the design of the website is pretty basic so it's a little boring to read this, because you get lost in the amounts of text that is written just in many paragraphs. Because of the amount of text it's not that easy for the user to understand the bug so he needs to read it couple of times until he gets it. Also, The solution this site suggests is described very shortly for people who are more expirienced in this field of bugs. The user is not able to leave a comment or to ask for explanation becase the site is just a source of information which makes it harder for the user.

In Web 3.0 on the other hand, The information is much wider, a lot of people are leaving comments and adding new information based on their expirience with the bug. So if the user is struggeling in understanding, he can leave a comment, and even in the same day somebody may answer him and make it more clear. Also, there are many different solutions that are fixing the bug, so if the user didn't get the first solution, he can try another one that he understands better. The website is much more alive, the design is better for the eyes, The website is not just a page with text but it's more alive while you are reading it. This website is so alive that is has a Gif that shows an example of the POC of the bug, which helps the user to understand it much faster and better.
